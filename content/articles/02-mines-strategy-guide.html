Mine Count Strategies

Low Risk: 1-3 Mines

  • Higher probability of finding gems
  • Lower multipliers per gem
  • Good for steady, smaller wins
  • Recommended for beginners

Medium Risk: 4-10 Mines

  • Balanced risk-reward ratio
  • Moderate multipliers
  • More strategic depth
  • Popular choice among regular players

High Risk: 11-24 Mines

  • Very low probability of success
  • Extremely high multipliers
  • High variance gameplay
  • For experienced players only

When to Cash Out

The key decision in Mines is knowing when to collect your winnings:

Early Cash Out (1-3 gems)

  • Higher success rate
  • Smaller profits
  • Good for building balance gradually

Mid-Game Cash Out (4-8 gems)

  • Moderate risk/reward balance
  • Decent multipliers achieved
  • Common stopping point

Late Cash Out (9+ gems)

  • Low probability of reaching
  • High multipliers
  • Risk losing everything

Tile Selection: Does It Matter?

Short answer: No.

Mines uses provably fair algorithms where mine positions are determined before you start clicking. Whether you click corners, edges, or center tiles, your odds are identical.

Bankroll Management

The 1% Rule

Never bet more than 1% of your total bankroll on a single game. This ensures you can survive losing streaks.

Session Limits

  • Win Limit: Stop when up 20-50% of session budget
  • Loss Limit: Stop when down 50% of session budget
  • Time Limit: Set a timer for 30-60 minutes

The Mathematics of the House Edge

Mines has a 3% house edge, meaning:

  • For every $100 wagered, expect to lose $3 on average
  • Short-term results vary wildly
  • Long-term, the house always wins
