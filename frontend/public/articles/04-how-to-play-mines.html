Introduction

Welcome to Mines! If you are new to this exciting grid-based game from Spribe, you have come to the right place. This beginner's guide will walk you through everything you need to know to start playing Mines with confidence.

Mines is one of the simplest casino games to learn, drawing inspiration from the classic Minesweeper computer game. However, unlike Minesweeper, there is no logic puzzle to solve. Instead, it is a game of chance where you reveal tiles hoping to find gems while avoiding hidden mines.

By the end of this guide, you will understand how to place bets, choose your risk level, reveal tiles, and most importantly, when to cash out. For a deeper understanding of the game's mechanics, check out our What is Mines? comprehensive guide.

Game Overview

Before diving into the details, let us establish what you are working with:

The Basics

  • Grid: 5x5 tiles (25 total positions)
  • Mines: You choose 1-24 mines to hide on the grid
  • Gems: Remaining tiles contain gems (25 minus mines)
  • Goal: Reveal gems without hitting a mine
  • Win Condition: Cash out before hitting a mine
  • Lose Condition: Click on a mine

What Makes Mines Special

  • Player Control: You decide when to stop and cash out
  • Adjustable Risk: Choose your own risk level with mine count
  • Instant Results: No waiting for spins or animations
  • Progressive Multipliers: Rewards increase with each gem found
  • Simple Rules: Easy to learn in minutes

RTP and House Edge

Mines has an RTP (Return to Player) of approximately 97%, meaning a 3% house edge. This is competitive with many casino games. For detailed information, see our Mines RTP Explained article.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here is how to play Mines from start to finish:

Step 1: Set Your Bet Amount

Before starting a round, decide how much you want to wager.

  • Use the +/- buttons or input field to set your bet
  • Most platforms offer minimum and maximum bet limits
  • Start small while learning the game
  • Never bet more than you can afford to lose

Step 2: Choose Your Mine Count

Select how many mines will be hidden on the grid (1-24).

  • Fewer mines (1-5): Safer, smaller multipliers
  • Medium mines (6-12): Balanced risk and reward
  • More mines (13-24): Riskier, larger multipliers

Beginners should start with 3-5 mines to get comfortable with the game.

Step 3: Start the Round

Click on any tile on the grid to begin. Your bet is placed when you make your first click.

Step 4: Reveal Tiles

Click on tiles one at a time. Each click reveals either:

  • Gem (Star): You are safe! Your multiplier increases
  • Mine: Game over, you lose your bet

Step 5: Decide: Cash Out or Continue

After each gem, you have a choice:

  • Cash Out: Collect your current winnings (bet x multiplier)
  • Continue: Click another tile to increase your multiplier

Step 6: Round Ends

The round ends when you either:

  • Cash out (you win your bet times the multiplier)
  • Hit a mine (you lose your bet)
  • Reveal all gems (automatic cash out at maximum multiplier)

Understanding the Interface

The Mines interface is designed to be intuitive. Here is what you will see:

Main Game Area

  • The Grid: 5x5 arrangement of tiles in the center
  • Unrevealed Tiles: Usually shown as blank or with a question mark
  • Gems: Displayed as stars or diamonds when revealed
  • Mines: Shown as bombs when the round ends

Control Panel

  • Bet Amount: Shows and allows adjustment of your wager
  • Mine Selector: Choose number of mines (1-24)
  • Current Multiplier: Your potential payout multiplier
  • Cash Out Button: Click to collect winnings
  • Potential Win: Shows bet x current multiplier

Information Displays

  • Balance: Your current account balance
  • Bet History: Record of previous rounds
  • Game Rules: Usually accessible via info button

Bet Settings Explained

Understanding betting options helps you manage your bankroll effectively.

Bet Amount

This is how much you wager per round. Consider:

  • Start with 1-2% of your total bankroll per bet
  • This allows you to survive losing streaks
  • Adjust based on your risk tolerance

Quick Bet Buttons

Many platforms offer quick buttons like:

  • Min: Sets the minimum bet
  • Max: Sets the maximum bet (use with extreme caution)
  • 1/2: Halves your current bet
  • 2x: Doubles your current bet

Betting Best Practices

  • Set a session budget before playing
  • Never increase bets to chase losses
  • Consider your total session length when setting bet size
  • Higher bets do not improve your odds

During Gameplay

Once the round starts, here is what happens and what to consider:

Your First Click

  • Click any tile on the grid
  • Position does not matter since mine placement is random
  • Do not overthink which tile to click

After Finding a Gem

When you reveal a gem:

  • Your multiplier increases
  • The "Cash Out" button becomes active
  • You can see your potential winnings
  • The probability of the next click changes

Tracking Your Progress

Pay attention to:

  • Current Multiplier: What you would get if you cash out now
  • Gems Revealed: How many safe tiles you have found
  • Remaining Tiles: How many choices remain

The Tension Builds

As you reveal more gems:

  • Multipliers grow faster
  • But the probability of hitting a mine may increase
  • The psychological pressure to cash out intensifies
  • This is where discipline matters most

When to Cash Out

The cash out decision is the most important aspect of playing Mines.

Cash Out Strategies

  • Fixed Target: Set a multiplier goal before starting (e.g., cash out at 2x)
  • Fixed Gems: Decide how many gems to reveal before cashing out
  • Gut Feeling: Less recommended, as emotions can lead to poor decisions

Factors to Consider

  • Current multiplier vs. probability of next gem
  • Your session goals and profit targets
  • Your remaining bankroll
  • Whether you have reached your win goal

The Mathematics of Cashing Out

From a purely mathematical standpoint:

  • The expected value is the same whenever you cash out
  • The house edge is built into every multiplier
  • There is no "optimal" cash out point mathematically
  • Your decision should be based on your risk tolerance and bankroll

Common Cash Out Mistakes

  • Getting Greedy: Pushing too far and losing everything
  • Cashing Out Too Early: Never building meaningful multipliers
  • Inconsistency: Changing strategy mid-session based on emotions

Auto-Play Features

Some Mines platforms offer auto-play features. Here is what you need to know:

Random Reveal

Some versions offer a button that randomly reveals a tile for you.

  • Does not change your odds
  • Removes decision-making from tile selection
  • Convenient but also removes some engagement

Auto Cash Out

Set a multiplier target and the game automatically cashes out when reached.

  • Removes temptation to continue past your target
  • Helps maintain discipline
  • Can be a responsible gambling tool

Auto Bet

Some platforms allow automated betting rounds.

  • Warning: This can lead to rapid bankroll depletion
  • Not recommended for beginners
  • If used, set strict limits
  • Monitor your balance frequently

Caution with Auto Features

Automated features can make it easier to lose track of:

  • How much you have wagered
  • How much you have lost
  • How long you have been playing

Always maintain awareness of your gambling activity.

Common Beginner Mistakes

Avoid these typical errors that new players make:

Mistake 1: Starting with High Mine Counts

Why it happens: The allure of big multipliers is tempting.

Why it is wrong: You will lose most rounds, depleting your bankroll before understanding the game.

Solution: Start with 3-5 mines until you are comfortable.

Mistake 2: Betting Too Large

Why it happens: Excitement or desire to win big quickly.

Why it is wrong: Large bets with gambling variance can wipe out your bankroll fast.

Solution: Bet 1-2% of your session bankroll per round.

Mistake 3: Chasing Losses

Why it happens: Emotional reaction to losing streaks.

Why it is wrong: Increasing bets after losses accelerates losses.

Solution: Stick to your predetermined bet size regardless of results.

Mistake 4: No Cash Out Plan

Why it happens: Not thinking ahead before starting.

Why it is wrong: In-the-moment decisions are often emotional and poor.

Solution: Decide your cash out target before each round.

Mistake 5: Playing Without Limits

Why it happens: Getting caught up in the excitement.

Why it is wrong: Can lead to significant financial and emotional harm.

Solution: Set time and money limits before playing.

Mistake 6: Believing in Patterns

Why it happens: Human brains seek patterns everywhere.

Why it is wrong: Each round is completely random and independent.

Solution: Accept that past results do not predict future outcomes.

Practice Tips

Before playing with real money, consider these practice suggestions:

Demo Mode

Many platforms offer free demo or practice mode:

  • Uses virtual credits instead of real money
  • Identical gameplay to real mode
  • Perfect for learning the interface
  • Great for testing different mine counts

Start Small

When transitioning to real money:

  • Use minimum bets for your first sessions
  • Focus on learning, not winning
  • Observe how variance affects your bankroll

Track Your Sessions

Consider keeping a simple log:

  • Starting bankroll
  • Ending bankroll
  • Number of rounds played
  • Mine count used
  • How you felt during and after playing

Set Learning Goals

Rather than profit goals, set learning objectives:

  • "I will stick to my cash out target for 20 rounds"
  • "I will not increase my bet after a loss"
  • "I will stop after my predetermined time limit"

Responsible Gambling

Playing responsibly is essential for an enjoyable experience.

Before You Play

  • Set a budget you can afford to lose entirely
  • Set a time limit for your session
  • Never gamble with rent, bills, or essential money
  • Never gamble to solve financial problems

While Playing

  • Stick to your predetermined limits
  • Take regular breaks
  • Do not drink alcohol while gambling
  • Stop if you are feeling frustrated or emotional

After Playing

  • Review your session objectively
  • Accept losses as the cost of entertainment
  • Do not try to win back losses in another session

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

  • Gambling more than you can afford
  • Neglecting responsibilities to gamble
  • Lying about gambling activities
  • Chasing losses
  • Feeling anxious or irritable when not gambling
  • Borrowing money to gamble
