Probability Basics

Before diving into Mines-specific calculations, let us establish some fundamental probability concepts.

What is Probability?

Probability is a measure of how likely an event is to occur, expressed as a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain). It can also be expressed as a percentage (0% to 100%).

Probability = Favorable Outcomes / Total Possible Outcomes

The Mines Grid

The standard Mines grid consists of:

  • Total Tiles: 25 (5x5 grid)
  • Mines: Variable, chosen by player (1-24)
  • Gems: 25 minus number of mines

Independent vs. Dependent Events

In Mines, each click is a dependent event, meaning the probability changes based on previous outcomes:

  • When you reveal a gem, there are fewer gems remaining
  • The total number of unrevealed tiles also decreases
  • The probability of the next click being safe changes accordingly

This is different from games like roulette, where each spin is independent and probabilities remain constant.

Single Click Probability

The probability of finding a gem on any single click can be calculated using a simple formula:

P(Gem) = Gems Remaining / Tiles Remaining

Changing Probabilities

After revealing a gem, the probabilities shift. For example, with 5 mines (20 gems):

  • Click 1: 20/25 = 80.00% (20 gems, 25 tiles)
  • Click 2: 19/24 = 79.17% (19 gems, 24 tiles)
  • Click 3: 18/23 = 78.26% (18 gems, 23 tiles)
  • Click 4: 17/22 = 77.27% (17 gems, 22 tiles)
  • Click 5: 16/21 = 76.19% (16 gems, 21 tiles)

Multiple Click Probability

To calculate the probability of surviving multiple clicks, multiply the individual probabilities together.

The Multiplication Rule

For dependent events:

P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B|A)

Where P(B|A) is the probability of B occurring given that A has occurred.

Example: 5 Mines, Surviving 3 Clicks

Probability of finding 3 consecutive gems with 5 mines:

P(3 gems) = P(1st gem) × P(2nd gem | 1st gem) × P(3rd gem | 1st and 2nd gems)
P(3 gems) = (20/25) × (19/24) × (18/23)
P(3 gems) = 0.80 × 0.7917 × 0.7826
P(3 gems) = 0.496 or 49.6%

Survival Probability Formula

For k successful reveals with M mines:

P(k gems) = [(25-M)! / (25-M-k)!] / [25! / (25-k)!]

This simplifies to multiplying consecutive fractions.

Expected Value Calculations

Expected Value (EV) tells you the average outcome you can expect per bet over the long run.

EV Formula

EV = (P(Win) × Payout) - (P(Loss) × Stake)

Mines EV Analysis

Due to the 97% RTP (3% house edge), the expected value of any bet in Mines is:

EV = -0.03 × Stake

For every $1 bet, you expect to lose $0.03 on average.

Optimal Cash Out Points

While there is no mathematically superior cash out point (EV is constant), you can analyze different approaches based on your goals.

Risk-Adjusted Thinking

Consider the risk-to-reward ratio at each decision point:

  • Current multiplier: ~1.69x
  • Probability of next gem: 17/22 = 77.27%
  • You risk losing ~1.69x to gain approximately 0.30x more
  • Risk/Reward: Risking ~5.6x the potential gain

15 Mines: After Revealing 2 Gems

  • Current multiplier: ~5.85x
  • Probability of next gem: 8/23 = 34.78%
  • You risk losing ~5.85x to gain approximately 8.45x more
  • Risk/Reward: Risking ~0.69x the potential gain

Variance Considerations

While EV is identical, variance affects your actual experience:

  • Early cash out: More frequent small wins, smoother bankroll
  • Late cash out: Rare large wins, volatile bankroll

Neither approach is mathematically superior, they just offer different experiences with the same expected loss.

Variance Analysis

Variance measures how much your actual results will deviate from the expected value.

Standard Deviation in Mines

Higher mine counts and later cash outs produce higher variance. This means:

  • Bigger winning sessions are possible
  • Bigger losing sessions are also more likely
  • Results become less predictable

Practical Variance Impact

Consider two 100-round sessions with identical EV:

Low Variance (3 mines, cash out after 2 gems)

  • Expected wins: ~77 rounds
  • Typical session range: -10% to +15% of bankroll
  • Predictable, steady experience

High Variance (15 mines, cash out after 3 gems)

  • Expected wins: ~5 rounds
  • Typical session range: -100% to +200% of bankroll
  • Volatile, unpredictable experience

Bankroll Requirements

Higher variance requires larger bankrolls to sustain the same number of rounds. If you want to play 100 rounds:

  • Low variance: 30-50 bet units may suffice
  • High variance: 100+ bet units recommended

Practical Examples

Let us work through some real scenarios to solidify your understanding.

Example 1: Calculating Your Odds

Scenario: You select 7 mines and have already revealed 4 gems. What is the probability of your next click being safe?

Solution:

  • Original gems: 25 - 7 = 18
  • Gems revealed: 4
  • Remaining gems: 18 - 4 = 14
  • Remaining tiles: 25 - 4 = 21
  • P(gem) = 14/21 = 66.67%

Example 2: Survival Probability

Scenario: With 10 mines, what is the probability of successfully revealing 5 gems?

Solution:

P(5 gems) = (15/25) × (14/24) × (13/23) × (12/22) × (11/21)
P(5 gems) = 0.60 × 0.583 × 0.565 × 0.545 × 0.524
P(5 gems) = 0.0559 or 5.59%

Example 3: Risk Assessment

Scenario: You have a $100 bankroll and want to make $5 bets. With 5 mines, what is the probability of going bust in 20 rounds if you cash out after 1 gem each time?

Solution:

  • Each round: 80% chance of ~1.19x return, 20% chance of loss
  • Going bust requires 20 consecutive losses (losing all 20 rounds)
  • P(20 losses) = 0.20^20 = essentially 0
  • But losing 15 out of 20 rounds is possible and would significantly deplete bankroll

The probability calculation for partial bankroll loss is more complex and depends on the specific sequence of wins and losses.

Common Statistical Mistakes

Understanding probability also means avoiding common errors in thinking.

The Gambler's Fallacy

Mistake: "I have lost 5 rounds in a row, so I am due for a win."

Reality: Each round is independent. Past losses do not affect future probabilities. The dice, cards, and RNG have no memory.

Hot and Cold Tile Fallacy

Mistake: "The top-left corner has been safe in my last few games, so I will start there."

Reality: Mine positions are randomly determined each round. Position history has no predictive value.

Confusion About RTP

Mistake: "The game has 97% RTP, so I should get back $97 of every $100 I bet."

Reality: RTP is a long-term statistical average across millions of rounds, not a per-session guarantee. Individual sessions vary wildly.

Pattern Recognition

Mistake: "I notice that after two gems in a row on the right side, the third is usually on the left."

Reality: Human brains are wired to find patterns, even in random data. These perceived patterns have no predictive power.

Sunk Cost Fallacy

Mistake: "I have already revealed 8 gems in this high-risk round, I should continue because I have invested so much."

Reality: Your previous reveals are irrelevant to the current decision. Each click should be evaluated independently based on current probabilities and your multiplier.

Responsible Gambling

Understanding statistics is crucial for responsible gambling. The mathematics make one thing clear: the house always wins in the long run.

Key Statistical Realities

  • Expected value is negative on every bet (-3%)
  • No strategy can overcome the house edge
  • Short-term wins are possible but not sustainable
  • The longer you play, the more certain your losses become

Healthy Approach

  • View gambling as paid entertainment, not investment
  • Set strict loss limits before playing
  • Never chase losses with larger bets
  • Understand that past results do not predict future outcomes
