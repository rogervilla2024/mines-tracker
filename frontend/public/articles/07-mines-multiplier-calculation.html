Introduction

Every time you reveal a gem in Mines, your multiplier increases. But have you ever wondered exactly how those multipliers are calculated? Why does revealing one gem with 5 mines give you 1.19x, while revealing one gem with 20 mines gives you 4.75x?

The answer lies in probability mathematics and the integration of the house edge. Understanding these calculations will not help you "beat" the game (that is impossible), but it will give you a deeper appreciation for how Mines works and why multipliers are set the way they are.

This guide will walk you through the mathematical formulas behind Mines multipliers, with detailed examples and complete multiplier tables for reference. For broader context, see our What is Mines? guide and Statistics & Probabilities article.

Fair Multiplier Concept

To understand actual multipliers, we first need to understand what a "fair" multiplier would be.

What is a Fair Multiplier?

A fair multiplier is one that would result in zero expected profit or loss for either the player or the house. It is the mathematically "break-even" payout.

Fair Multiplier Formula

Fair Multiplier = 1 / Probability of Success

Example: First Click with 5 Mines

  • Total tiles: 25
  • Mines: 5
  • Gems: 20
  • Probability of gem: 20/25 = 0.80 (80%)
  • Fair Multiplier: 1 / 0.80 = 1.25x

Why Fair Multipliers Cannot Exist in Casinos

If casinos offered fair multipliers, they would make zero profit. To sustain operations, casinos must reduce multipliers slightly below fair value, creating the "house edge."

Integrating House Edge

Mines has an RTP of approximately 97%, meaning a house edge of about 3%. This edge is integrated into every multiplier.

House Edge Application

Actual Multiplier = Fair Multiplier × RTP
Actual Multiplier = Fair Multiplier × 0.97

Example: First Click with 5 Mines (with House Edge)

  • Fair Multiplier: 1.25x
  • RTP: 97% (0.97)
  • Actual Multiplier: 1.25 × 0.97 = 1.21x

The Effect of House Edge

For every multiplier in the game:

  • The displayed multiplier is ~3% lower than fair value
  • This reduction accumulates across multiple reveals
  • The house edge applies regardless of mine count or strategy

The Multiplier Formula

Let us formalize the multiplier calculation for any number of gems revealed.

Variables

  • M = Number of mines on grid
  • G = Number of gems (25 - M)
  • k = Number of gems already revealed
  • RTP = Return to Player (0.97)

Cumulative Probability After k Reveals

P(k gems) = [G × (G-1) × (G-2) × ... × (G-k+1)] / [25 × 24 × 23 × ... × (25-k+1)]

This can be written more compactly as:

P(k gems) = [G! / (G-k)!] / [25! / (25-k)!]

Multiplier After k Reveals

Multiplier(k) = RTP / P(k gems)
Multiplier(k) = 0.97 / P(k gems)

Simplified Formula

For practical calculation, multiply the successive probabilities:

Multiplier(k) = 0.97 × (25/G) × (24/(G-1)) × (23/(G-2)) × ... × ((25-k+1)/(G-k+1))

Step-by-Step Calculations

Let us work through detailed examples to solidify understanding.

Example 1: 5 Mines, Reveal 3 Gems

Setup: 5 mines, 20 gems, want multiplier after 3 gems

Step 1: Calculate Cumulative Probability

P(1st gem) = 20/25 = 0.800
P(2nd gem | 1st) = 19/24 = 0.792
P(3rd gem | 1st, 2nd) = 18/23 = 0.783

P(3 gems) = 0.800 × 0.792 × 0.783 = 0.496

Step 2: Calculate Multiplier

Multiplier = 0.97 / 0.496 = 1.96x

Result: After revealing 3 gems with 5 mines, multiplier is approximately 1.96x

Example 2: 10 Mines, Reveal 2 Gems

Setup: 10 mines, 15 gems, want multiplier after 2 gems

Step 1: Calculate Cumulative Probability

P(1st gem) = 15/25 = 0.600
P(2nd gem | 1st) = 14/24 = 0.583

P(2 gems) = 0.600 × 0.583 = 0.350

Step 2: Calculate Multiplier

Multiplier = 0.97 / 0.350 = 2.77x

Result: After revealing 2 gems with 10 mines, multiplier is approximately 2.77x

Example 3: 15 Mines, Reveal 1 Gem

Setup: 15 mines, 10 gems, want multiplier after 1 gem

Step 1: Calculate Probability

P(1 gem) = 10/25 = 0.400

Step 2: Calculate Multiplier

Multiplier = 0.97 / 0.400 = 2.43x

Result: After revealing 1 gem with 15 mines, multiplier is approximately 2.43x

Key Observations

  • Low mines: Multipliers grow slowly but steadily
  • High mines: Multipliers grow explosively but probability drops fast
  • The growth rate accelerates as fewer tiles remain
  • Early gems always provide smaller multiplier increases

Multiplier Growth Per Gem

Each successive gem increases the multiplier by a larger percentage:

  • With 5 mines, 1st gem: 1.21x (21% gain from 1.00x)
  • With 5 mines, 2nd gem: 1.53x (26% gain from 1.21x)
  • With 5 mines, 3rd gem: 1.96x (28% gain from 1.53x)

This accelerating growth creates the temptation to continue revealing gems.

Maximum Multiplier Paradox

Interestingly, 10 and 15 mines share the same maximum multiplier. This is because:

  • With 10 mines: Reveal 15 gems (60% of grid)
  • With 15 mines: Reveal 10 gems (40% of grid)
  • Both represent revealing all gems on the grid
  • The probability calculation becomes symmetrical

Practical Reality

These maximum multipliers are theoretically possible but practically unrealistic:

  • A 0.00003% event happens once in approximately 3.3 million attempts
  • Most players will never see maximum multipliers
  • The journey to maximum multiplier involves immense risk

Practical Examples

Let us apply multiplier understanding to real scenarios.

Scenario 1: Conservative Play

Setup: $10 bet, 3 mines, plan to cash out after 3 gems

  • Probability of success: 66.96%
  • Multiplier: 1.45x
  • Potential payout: $10 × 1.45 = $14.50
  • Potential profit: $4.50
  • Expected value: (0.6696 × $4.50) - (0.3304 × $10) = $0.01 - $3.30 = -$0.29

Note: The negative expected value confirms the house edge.

Scenario 2: Aggressive Play

Setup: $5 bet, 15 mines, cash out after 2 gems

  • Probability of success: 15.00%
  • Multiplier: 6.47x
  • Potential payout: $5 × 6.47 = $32.35
  • Potential profit: $27.35
  • Expected value: (0.15 × $27.35) - (0.85 × $5) = $4.10 - $4.25 = -$0.15

Scenario 3: Calculating When to Cash Out

Situation: 5 mines, already revealed 4 gems (current multiplier ~2.54x)

Question: What do I gain vs. risk by clicking one more?

  • Current tiles remaining: 21
  • Gems remaining: 16
  • Probability of 5th gem: 16/21 = 76.2%
  • Multiplier if successful: ~3.32x
  • Gain if successful: 3.32x - 2.54x = 0.78x additional
  • Loss if unsuccessful: 2.54x (entire current multiplier)

Risk/Reward: You risk 2.54x to gain 0.78x, with 76.2% success chance.

Why Understanding Multipliers Matters

Knowing how multipliers work has practical benefits.

Informed Decision Making

Understanding the math helps you:

  • Recognize why certain mine counts offer certain multipliers
  • Evaluate risk/reward at each decision point
  • Avoid irrational expectations

Detecting Fairness

If a platform's multipliers significantly differ from calculated values, it might indicate:

  • Different RTP than advertised
  • Potentially unfair game mechanics
  • Modified house edge

Accepting Reality

The math clearly shows:

  • Every multiplier favors the house by ~3%
  • No mine count or strategy changes this
  • Long-term profit is mathematically impossible

Conclusion

Mines multipliers are not arbitrary numbers. They are precisely calculated based on probability mathematics and designed to give the house a consistent edge.

Key Formulas

  • Cumulative Probability: Product of individual click probabilities
