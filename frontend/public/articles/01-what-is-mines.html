What is the Mines Game?

Mines is a grid-based casino game that presents players with a 5x5 grid containing 25 tiles. Hidden beneath these tiles are a combination of gems (or stars) and mines. The player's objective is simple: reveal as many gem tiles as possible without uncovering a mine.

The game's premise draws heavy inspiration from Microsoft's classic Minesweeper, a puzzle game that has been included with Windows operating systems since 1990. However, unlike Minesweeper, where players use logic clues to deduce mine locations, Mines is purely a game of chance. The positions of mines are determined by a random number generator (RNG) at the start of each round, and there are no hints or numbers to guide your selections.

Key Features of Mines

  • Grid Size: Standard 5x5 grid with 25 tiles
  • Customizable Mines: Players choose between 1-24 mines per round
  • Progressive Multipliers: Each safe tile revealed increases your potential payout
  • Cash Out Anytime: Secure your winnings at any point during the round
  • Provably Fair: Results can be independently verified
  • Instant Results: No waiting for spins or draws

The beauty of Mines lies in its simplicity. There are no complex rules to memorize, no bonus rounds with convoluted mechanics, and no need to wait for outcomes. Each click reveals a tile instantly, and you immediately see whether your luck has held or if you have struck a mine.

How Mines Works: Core Mechanics

Understanding the core mechanics of Mines is essential before you begin playing. The game follows a straightforward sequence that repeats with each round:

Before each round begins, you must decide how much you want to wager. Most platforms offer a wide range of betting options, from minimal amounts suitable for casual players to higher stakes for more experienced gamblers. Always remember to bet only what you can afford to lose.

After revealing each gem, you face a decision: cash out and secure your current winnings, or continue revealing tiles to increase your multiplier. This risk-reward dynamic is at the heart of what makes Mines so engaging.

Mine Distribution

When you start a round, the game's RNG randomly distributes the selected number of mines across the grid. Each position has an equal probability of containing a mine. This means:

  • With 1 mine: Each tile has a 1/25 (4%) chance of being a mine
  • With 5 mines: Each tile has a 5/25 (20%) chance of being a mine
  • With 12 mines: Each tile has a 12/25 (48%) chance of being a mine
  • With 24 mines: Each tile has a 24/25 (96%) chance of being a mine

The remaining tiles contain gems. With 5 mines selected, for example, 20 tiles will contain gems (80% of the grid).

Understanding Multipliers

Multipliers in Mines are not arbitrary numbers, they are calculated based on mathematical probability. The multiplier for each successful reveal reflects the risk you have taken. Learn more in our comprehensive Multiplier Calculation guide.

How Multipliers Are Calculated

The basic formula for multiplier calculation involves the probability of success. If there is a 50% chance of revealing a gem, the fair multiplier would be 2x (before house edge). The actual multiplier will be slightly lower to account for the house edge.

Example Multiplier Progression

RTP and House Edge Overview

Return to Player (RTP) is a crucial concept for understanding any casino game. For a comprehensive explanation, see our Mines RTP Explained article.

Mines RTP: 97%

Mines offers an RTP of approximately 97%, making it one of the more favorable casino games available. This means that, theoretically, for every $100 wagered over the long term, $97 would be returned to players as winnings.

House Edge: 3%

The house edge in Mines is approximately 3%, which is lower than many traditional casino games. This edge is built into the multiplier calculations, ensuring each multiplier is slightly below what pure probability would dictate.

Important: RTP is a theoretical long-term figure calculated over millions of rounds. Individual sessions can vary dramatically. Never assume you are "due" for a win or that past results affect future outcomes.

Provably Fair Gaming

Spribe implements provably fair technology in Mines, allowing players to verify that game outcomes are genuinely random and not manipulated.

How Provably Fair Works

Before each round, the game generates a server seed that determines mine positions. This seed is hashed and shown to the player before they place their bet. After the round, players can use the revealed seed to verify that the mine positions were predetermined and not changed during gameplay.
